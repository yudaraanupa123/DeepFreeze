LITERATURE SURVEY


From manufacturing and transportation to project management and computer systems, scheduling optimization is a challenging problem that affects a wide range of businesses. Constraint solvers have become a powerful tool for navigating the complex landscape of constraints that are inherent in scheduling as a result of the major evolution of the computational approaches used to address these problems [1]. This study of the literature examines the state-of-the-art regarding the complex interaction between system constraints, algorithmic decisions, and the efficiency of constraint solvers when used to optimize schedules. This study examines previous studies in an effort to provide light on the complex interactions that affect how efficient and successful these solutions are.


In order to constraint solvers to be efficient and effective in tackling schedule optimization problems, the choice of suitable algorithms is crucial. The choice of algorithm is an important factor in the performance of solvers because different algorithms exhibit different strengths and weaknesses. Scheduling issues have been tackled by methods including constraint programming, mixed integer programming, and heuristic approaches, each of which targets a different part of the issues. For example, mixed integer programming excels at capturing complex dependencies inside scheduling issues, whereas constraint programming provides a flexible framework for modeling sophisticated constraints [2]. The choice of algorithm must be carefully considered based on the nature of the problem and the constraints imposed. 2.2 System Constraints and Solver Behavior


When used in scheduling optimization, system constraints cover a range of elements that affect how constraint solvers behave [3]. These limitations include the amount of computational capacity, the amount of memory, the processing speed, and the difficulty of the particular issue 2.4 Solver Performance Analysis To evaluate the effectiveness of various constraint solvers in particular scheduling scenarios, numerous empirical studies have been carried out. Comparative analyses of mixed integer programming solvers SCIP and CBC against Optaplanner and MiniCP have revealed information about their effectiveness and applicability for various problem types. Researchers and practitioners can learn how different solvers perform under various system constraints by using these assessments, which often quantify characteristics like solution quality, runtime, and scalability. When choosing solvers and algorithms for particular schedule optimization tasks, these evaluations help provide the empirical groundwork for wise choices. By focusing on these aspects, this work hopes to add to the corpus of knowledge that supports efficient constraint satisfaction and optimization procedures in real-world settings

Research Problem


During the past few decades dramatic technological improvement has occurred in many fields like education, administration, mass media, health, agriculture etc. With the help of technology, a rapid increment in productivity and efficiency can be seen. Nevertheless, hundreds of millions of rural farmers in less developed areas still live-in poverty and without any means of social technological interactions with the society. Rather than using new technology those people are still stuck in the old traditional ways. Technology and world are always updating. There is always something new in every second. Without being connected with the world these poor people face many problems. For an example from day-by-day new trends of agriculture may be popped out. New diseases in the cultivation or cattle may be invented. But the ones who are not familiar with technology with not get any update. Not only that the focus nowadays is infectious diseases around everywhere. According to the surveys conducted by agricultural specialists they state that an agricultural disease may affect not only the harvest where the symptoms appeared it may damage other farming and spread to the level which it can damage the economy and public health . The other reason is to equip the farmers with fertilizer utilization processes and to give knowledge about fertilizers. Since the fertilizer act a main role in cultivation first thing is to make soil condition to initiate cultivation and fertilizer is the driving force to have a better soil state.


The main reason for these problems is not having an idea of what is coming ahead. It is not about predicting nor future reading. Just they do not have a method to get current or upcoming facts on agriculture and farming. To address these problems new technological approaches must be introduced to them. A platform should be introduced to them to prevent and combat these diseases. There are around hundreds of farmers with a mind-blowing knowledge of every aspect regarding agriculture. But the truth is that they will not get a chance to deliver their skills and knowledge to the rest. And also, some rural people have so much interest in getting advice and consultations from experts in agricultural department, but they should spare a lot of money on that. There for a new technological trend should be implemented in them.


While we were conducting our research investigation, we discovered that about 80% of Sri Lankan farmers have no notion if the money given by the intermediate sellers between their crops is reasonable and how they'd earn money in the future. As per this reason, the above-mentioned number of farmers don't have a clear awareness about the factors that need to be concerned about price fluctuation when selling their crops to the intermediate sellers.


For Farmers, agriculture is their primary source of income. Also, the Sri Lankan economy relies heavily on agriculture. And the main target of the farmers is the profit of their harvest, and they are expecting a reasonable price for their crops. Predicting the income, outcome, and profit is very reliable since they have been gone through many difficulties and struggles. There are no sufficient facilities and applications developed for price prediction up to date in Sri Lanka. Sri Lankan farmers face many problems without being concerned about the environment and weather facts for their crops. Apart from that, farmers will face price fluctuation difficulties due to the impact of pandemic issues in the country. And without knowing the exact intermediate buying and selling prices, farmers will sell their harvest for a lesser amount of value. Due to the less profit gaining from their harvest, some farmers commit suicide, and some fall mentally down when they fail to sell their crops for a reasonable price.

There should be some means to ensure the suitable crop to be cultivated, price, and profit relevant to each province since the market price may vary from region to region. For a profitable and accurate outcome, data sets should be collected using the factors mentioned above. And also, the crops which are grown in the wet zone will not be successful in the dry zone and vice versa. Many people in Sri Lanka depend on farming; therefore, proper awareness and understanding must be provided for the farmers and encourage them not to end up losing their valuable cultivation.


Currently in Sri Lanka the department of agriculture is not using any technologies to identify insects, the damage done by them and to update the famers(users) they use radio, village conferences, television programs so the famers will not take these situations seriously and end up losing the crops. Government also would not take necessary steps to get update the famers because this process takes time and money.

Taking into account the aforementioned factors, the identified research problem is to create the most appropriate and correct model, datasets, and constraints to use in the application, as well as to find a real-time optimal way to identify pests/insects using the Categorical cross entropy loss function algorithm, while keeping the application and web application user friendly to the users(famers).
