LITERATURE SURVEY


From manufacturing and transportation to project management and computer systems, scheduling optimization is a challenging problem that affects a wide range of businesses. Constraint solvers have become a powerful tool for navigating the complex landscape of constraints that are inherent in scheduling as a result of the major evolution of the computational approaches used to address these problems [1]. This study of the literature examines the state-of-the-art regarding the complex interaction between system constraints, algorithmic decisions, and the efficiency of constraint solvers when used to optimize schedules. This study examines previous studies in an effort to provide light on the complex interactions that affect how efficient and successful these solutions are.


In order to constraint solvers to be efficient and effective in tackling schedule optimization problems, the choice of suitable algorithms is crucial. The choice of algorithm is an important factor in the performance of solvers because different algorithms exhibit different strengths and weaknesses. Scheduling issues have been tackled by methods including constraint programming, mixed integer programming, and heuristic approaches, each of which targets a different part of the issues. For example, mixed integer programming excels at capturing complex dependencies inside scheduling issues, whereas constraint programming provides a flexible framework for modeling sophisticated constraints [2]. The choice of algorithm must be carefully considered based on the nature of the problem and the constraints imposed. 2.2 System Constraints and Solver Behavior


When used in scheduling optimization, system constraints cover a range of elements that affect how constraint solvers behave [3]. These limitations include the amount of computational capacity, the amount of memory, the processing speed, and the difficulty of the particular issue 2.4 Solver Performance Analysis To evaluate the effectiveness of various constraint solvers in particular scheduling scenarios, numerous empirical studies have been carried out. Comparative analyses of mixed integer programming solvers SCIP and CBC against Optaplanner and MiniCP have revealed information about their effectiveness and applicability for various problem types. Researchers and practitioners can learn how different solvers perform under various system constraints by using these assessments, which often quantify characteristics like solution quality, runtime, and scalability. When choosing solvers and algorithms for particular schedule optimization tasks, these evaluations help provide the empirical groundwork for wise choices. By focusing on these aspects, this work hopes to add to the corpus of knowledge that supports efficient constraint satisfaction and optimization procedures in real-world settings

Solution

Open-source solvers, essential computational tools for optimization problems, are widely utilized in fields like operations research, supply chain management, scheduling, and logistics. These solvers, developed and maintained by a collaborative community, offer various features addressing a broad range of optimization challenges, including mathematical programming, constraint programming, global optimization, and multi-objective optimization. Notable open-source solver libraries like COIN-OR, GLPK, SCIP, and PuLP provide versatile interfaces for integration into various programming environments. Open-source solvers boast advantages such as low cost, transparency, community support, flexibility, and extensibility. They efficiently handle optimization problems through well-established algorithms like the simplex method for linear programming and the branch-and-bound method for mixed-integer programming. The transparency and accessibility of source code contribute to user empowerment, making open-source solvers valuable tools for overcoming complex problems, maximizing resource allocation, and facilitating data-driven decisions across diverse sectors. Continuous improvement, collaboration, and extensive documentation further enhance their utility in diverse optimization applications..


Research Objectives

Technisian Dispatching with Linear programming and Mixed Integer Programming

Dispatch scheduling, a complex task involving efficient assignment of jobs to workers or vehicles while considering various constraints, is effectively addressed by Mixed Integer Programming (MIP). MIP formulates the dispatch scheduling problem as an optimization task, where tasks' start and end times, along with resource assignments, are decision variables. The objective function aims to minimize the overall time for completing all jobs within specified limitations. Binary variables represent potential task-resource assignments, subject to constraints ensuring each task is assigned to exactly one resource, resource exclusivity, and alignment of task durations with start and finish times. The MIP solver employs cutting-plane and branch-and-bound algorithms, strategically pruning the search space to find the optimal solution. This approach transforms dispatch scheduling complexities into a mathematical optimization effort, enhancing efficiency in navigating intricate constraints and task-resource allocations. .

Methodology



Dispatch scheduling poses a challenging task of efficiently assigning tasks to resources within specified constraints. Mixed Integer Programming (MIP) serves as an effective tool for addressing dispatch scheduling issues. In this problem, each task has a set completion time, date, and required resources with distinct capabilities and availability. MIP models dispatch scheduling as a mathematical optimization problem, defining decision variables for start and finish times of tasks and task-resource assignments. Binary variables represent potential task-resource assignments, subject to constraints ensuring each task is assigned to one resource, each resource handles one task at a time, and task timings align with duration and resource availability. MIP solvers, using methods like cutting plane and branch and bound algorithms, seek the optimal solution by exploring task-resource allocations and start timings while pruning the search space for efficiency. Ultimately, MIP-based dispatch scheduling optimizes overall task completion time under diverse constraints.


Mixed Integer Programming (MIP) serves as a powerful approach for achieving optimal solutions in work task scheduling by efficiently allocating assignments to workers. In the planning of project tasks, MIP aims to minimize the overall time, workforce requirements, or costs associated with task completion. This method involves formulating a mathematical model that encompasses various parameters and constraints characterizing the scheduling problem. The objective function, typically centered on reducing time or cost, guides the optimization process. Decision variables, representing factors like start and finish times, contribute to determining the best solution. Constraints encompass aspects such as personnel availability, required qualifications, and task deadlines. The MIP solver then identifies the optimal solution that adheres to constraints and minimizes the objective function. Overall, MIP proves to be a versatile tool, customizable to specific industries and organizations, offering an effective means to optimize task allocation, minimize completion time, and reduce costs in work task scheduling applications..
